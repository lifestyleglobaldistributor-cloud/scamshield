üõí #1 Online Shopping Scam

CRITICAL THREAT Facebook/Instagram

How it works: Two-phase attack: (1) Fake Facebook pages pretending to be legitimate stores, and (2) Real sellers sending wrong, damaged, or counterfeit items. Surge after 11.11, Black Friday, 12.12 sales.

Red flags: Too-good prices, new accounts, no reviews, requests for direct bank transfer, rush to buy.

Protection: Verify seller legitimacy, check reviews from multiple sources, use secure payment methods with buyer protection, screenshot all conversations.

üì¶ #2 Fake Delivery Scam

EVOLVING THREAT SMS/COD

How it works: No longer just phishing links. Now using COD (cash-on-delivery) services, victimizing both customers and riders through "pabili" services. Fake delivery notifications asking to click links or pay fees.

Red flags: Unexpected delivery notices, links in SMS, requests for payment before delivery, unknown tracking numbers.

Protection: Track orders only through official courier websites (type URL manually), verify with sender, never click SMS links, inspect COD packages before paying.

üìû #3 Call/Vishing Scam

74% SURGE Q1 2025 351,699 cases

Alarming data: 74% increase Q1 2025 (108,157 to 351,699 cases). Remnants of dismantled POGO operations now conducting scam calls. Well-trained groups of 7-10 people using AI voice changers.

Top types: Loan offers (55%), VIP rewards (24%), collections (10%), transaction verification (5%), job applications (3%), delivery (3%).

Red flags: Unsolicited calls from banks/government, urgency/threats, requests for OTP/PIN/account details, too-good-to-be-true offers.

Protection: Hang up immediately, call official numbers yourself, never share OTP/PIN, banks/government won't demand payment over phone, use caller ID apps like Whoscall.

üíº #4 Task/Job Scam

HIGH RISK Viber/WhatsApp

How it works: Promise easy money for simple tasks (liking posts, watching videos, app reviews). Start with small payments to build trust. Eventually ask for "investment" or upfront payment to unlock higher earnings. Circulating on Viber and WhatsApp.

Red flags: Unrealistic earnings (‚Ç±5,000+ for simple tasks), upfront payment required, pressure to recruit others, cryptocurrency payments.

Protection: Legitimate jobs don't require payment, verify company with SEC/DTI, be suspicious of guaranteed high income, research company online thoroughly.

üìà #5 Investment Scam

‚Ç±100B+ losses 2024 Ponzi/Pyramid

How it works: Promise guaranteed high returns (12% weekly, 48% monthly) with little/no risk. Often Ponzi or pyramid schemes. Heavy targeting of OFWs and professionals through social media and WhatsApp groups.

2024 losses: Over ‚Ç±100 billion, especially among OFWs and working professionals.

Red flags: Guaranteed returns, pressure to invest quickly, recruitment commissions, unregistered with SEC, vague business model.

Protection: If too good to be true, it is. Check SEC registration at sec.gov.ph, understand that high returns = high risk, consult financial advisor, avoid pressure tactics.

‚ù§Ô∏è #6 Love/Romance Scam

Long-term scheme Dating apps

How it works: Build fake romantic relationship online over weeks/months. Gain deep trust through daily communication. Now using deepfake technology for video calls. Eventually request money for emergencies, travel, medical needs, business opportunities.

Targets: OFWs, retirees, emotionally vulnerable individuals, singles.

Red flags: Quick profession of love, can't meet in person, asks for money, shares sob stories, claims to be overseas military/professional.

Protection: Never send money to someone you haven't met, verify identity via multiple video calls, reverse image search their photos, be wary of financial requests.

üí∞ #7 Loan Scam

55% of scam calls SMS/Apps

How it works: Unsolicited loan offers via call, text, social media. Promise instant approval with no requirements. Require upfront fees for "processing," "insurance," or "release." Loan never materializes. 55% of all scam calls are loan-related.

Red flags: Unsolicited offers, guaranteed approval, upfront payment required, pressure for immediate decision, SMS with clickable links.

Protection: Legitimate lenders don't ask for advance payment, verify with BSP registered lenders list, read all terms carefully, never click SMS loan links.

üë§ #8 Impersonation Scam

Government/Banks

How it works: Impersonate government officials (BIR, NBI, CICC, BOC), bank employees, company executives, or family members. Use AI voice cloning for convincing calls. Threaten legal action, claim account issues, or create urgent family emergencies to extract money or information.

Targets: All demographics, especially seniors and professionals.

Protection: Establish family code words, verify through official channels, government agencies don't demand immediate payment, call back on known official numbers, be suspicious of urgency.

‚úàÔ∏è #9 Travel Scam

Holiday surge Booking sites

How it works: Fake travel booking websites, too-good-to-be-true packages, non-existent accommodations. Spike during Christmas and summer vacation planning. Fake social media ads for hotel/flight deals.

Red flags: Prices significantly below market rate, payment only via wire transfer, new/suspicious websites, no contact information, pressure to book immediately.

Protection: Book through verified travel agencies (DOT accredited), use credit cards for protection, verify hotel/airline directly, research company reviews, get written confirmation.

üôè #10 Charity Scam

Exploits generosity

How it works: Fake charity appeals on social media, viral medical fundraising posts where money doesn't reach victims, impersonation of legitimate charities. Exploit Filipino generosity especially during Christmas.

Red flags: Only accepts direct bank transfers, no official charity registration, aggressive emotional appeals, unverified medical cases.

Protection: Donate through verified organizations only, check DSWD or LGU validation, verify charity registration with SEC, be wary of direct bank transfers to individuals, ask for receipts.

üîÑ #11 Middleman Scam

Gadget reselling

How it works: Common in gadget reselling and online transactions. Fake "middleman" or broker claims to facilitate safe transactions between buyer and seller. Takes payment from both parties and disappears.

Red flags: Unknown middleman, requests payment before delivery, no physical meetup, pressure to use specific payment method.

Protection: Use established platforms with buyer protection (Lazada, Shopee), meet in person for high-value items, use COD when possible, verify broker identity and reviews.

üé∞ #12 Online Gambling Scam

POGO-related

How it works: Illegal online gambling sites that rig games, refuse to pay winnings, or steal deposits. Related to banned POGO operations. Promise easy money but games are programmed to make you lose.

Red flags: Guaranteed wins, unregistered sites, requires large deposits, won't allow withdrawals, operates offshore.

Protection: Online gambling is illegal in Philippines except PAGCOR, avoid unlicensed sites, understand house always wins, seek help for gambling addiction at 1553 (NCMH).

ü§ñ AI-Powered Scams (NEW 2025)

EMERGING THREAT Deepfakes/Voice

How it works: Use AI to clone voices of family members or create deepfake videos requesting urgent money. ChatGPT and free AI tools make scams more convincing and accessible. Highly convincing impersonations of loved ones in distress.

Protection: Establish family code words, verify through multiple channels, be suspicious of urgent financial requests, call back on known numbers, ask personal questions only real family would know.

üè¢ POGO/Human Trafficking Scams

Life imprisonment Criminal operation

Recent case: Alice Guo (former Bamban mayor) convicted Nov 20, 2025 - sentenced to life imprisonment for running illegal scam compound where hundreds were forced to conduct online scams. Chinese national who falsely claimed Filipino citizenship.

How it works: Recruit workers for "online gaming" or "customer service" jobs abroad. Force them into scam operations. Often involves human trafficking, passport confiscation, physical abuse.

Protection: Research employers thoroughly, verify company registration, be wary of "too good" overseas job offers, report suspicious job postings to DOLE and CICC, never surrender passport to employer.

üì± SIM Swap/Palit SIM

Banking threat Telco

How it works: Scammers request SIM replacement from your telco provider pretending to be you. Gain access to your phone number. Reset banking passwords via SMS OTP. Drain bank accounts.

Protection: Register SIM under SIM Registration Act (RA 11934), set SIM PIN/PUK code, monitor unusual network activity (sudden loss of signal), use app-based 2FA (Google Authenticator) instead of SMS when possible, contact telco immediately if signal lost.

üé£ Phishing (Email/SMS)

19.2% fraud rate Banks/Gov

How it works: Fake emails/SMS from "banks," government agencies, or companies with malicious links. Steal passwords, credit card info, OTPs. Websites look identical to real ones.

Statistics: 19.2% suspected fraud rate in online communities (highest among industries analyzed).

Red flags: Urgent action required, suspicious sender address, spelling errors, requests for sensitive info, unexpected attachments/links.

Protection: Check sender email/number carefully, don't click suspicious links, type URLs manually, hover over links to see real destination, enable 2FA on all accounts, verify with company directly.

üí≥ Account Takeover (ATO)

‚Ç±409M losses 2024 Banking

How it works: Criminals gain unauthorized access to bank or e-wallet accounts by stealing login credentials through phishing, malware, or data breaches. Transfer money out before victim notices.

2024 impact: ‚Ç±409 million lost due to ATO-related fraud in Philippines alone.

Protection: Use strong unique passwords for each account, enable 2FA/biometrics, monitor transactions daily, set up transaction alerts, never save passwords in browser, log out after sessions, update apps regularly.

üí∏ Money Mule Recruitment

Criminal liability

How it works: Recruit you to transfer money through your account, promising commission. You become accomplice to money laundering. Your account used to move stolen funds.

Legal consequences: Can be charged with money laundering (RA 9160), face imprisonment and fines, permanent criminal record.

Protection: Never let others use your bank account/e-wallet, understand you can be criminally liable, report recruitment attempts to BSP and AMLC, legitimate work doesn't involve transferring others' money.

üí≥ Card Skimming

ATM/POS

How it works: Illegal devices attached to ATMs or payment terminals copy your card data and PIN. Used to create cloned cards or make unauthorized transactions.

Protection: Inspect ATMs for suspicious devices before use, cover keypad when entering PIN, use ATMs in well-lit secure locations (inside banks), monitor transactions immediately after ATM use, use chip/contactless over magnetic stripe, report suspicious ATMs to bank.

üé∞ Lottery/Prize Scam

Common

How it works: Claim you won lottery, raffle, or prize you never entered. Ask for processing fees, taxes, or personal info to claim prize that doesn't exist.

Red flags: Winning something you didn't enter, upfront payment required, urgency to claim, requests for bank details or ID copies.

Protection: You can't win what you didn't enter, legitimate lotteries don't require payment to claim (taxes deducted from winnings), verify with official PCSO only, never share bank details for "prize claims."
